A Guide to Goof for the Casual Connoiseur

This Canon has been grouped into five ranked "Goof" levels. Although the Committee of Carefully Considered Goofiness strives to ensure a fair goofiness assessment, this is of course based only on the Committee's personal opinions, and they'd like you to keep your own opinions entirely to yourself.

Goof - as defined by the Committee of Carefully Considered Goofiness - is a complex, multi-faceted quality. As you explore the different goof groupings (or goofings, as they are known in the academic literature) below, please feel free to question our judgement.

While the Committee would like to encourage the casual reviewer of the Canon to consider an epistemological dissection of the true nature of "goof" as it relates to visual media, for the sake of brevity and wit, we shall define it here thusly:

Goof = Silliness

Goofings

These goofings, or goof groupings, are ranked from Level 1 - Goofless to Level 5 - Gratuitous Goof.

A Level 1 movie will have virtually no goof. It probably belongs to either the genre "everything's bad all the time" or the "I'd never like to sleep with the lights off again" genre.

A Level 5 movie will have virtually non-stop goof. It will probably be absolutely hilarious. In fact, if you don't find Level 5 movies funny, you should probably have your sense of humor examined by a professional.

To get into the great world of judging other people's hard work by goofing movies, click on a Goof Level from the options below.
